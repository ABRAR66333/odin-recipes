Biryani

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish originating from South Asia. Known for its aromatic basmati rice, tender meat or vegetables, and a blend of essential spices, biryani is a delightful culinary experience.

ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, mutton, or vegetables
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • Whole spices: 2-3 bay leaves, 4-5 green cardamom pods, 4-5 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Rinse basmati rice and soak in water for 30 minutes. Drain.
  2. Boil water, add rice, and cook until 70-80% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a pan, heat oil/ghee, add sliced onions, and fry until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste, meat/vegetables, tomatoes, yogurt, green chilies, and spices. Cook until tomatoes soften.
  5. Layer partially cooked rice over the meat/vegetables. Add chopped coriander and mint leaves.
  6. Drizzle saffron-infused milk, and cover. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes.
  7. Fluff biryani with a fork before serving. Enjoy the aromatic biryani with raita or your preferred side dish!
